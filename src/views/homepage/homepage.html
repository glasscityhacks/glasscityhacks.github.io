Glass City Hacks

  • Toledo Ohio's First Hackathon
  • October 16-18, 2015
  • Toledo Museum of Art
Registration Opening Mid July

36 Hours. 500 Participants.
1 Awesome Event.

We want to bring together individuals in computer science, engineering, designers, artists, business, and other focuses for a weekend of fun, learning, and creativity.

Over the course of 36 hours, participants from here and beyond will embark on a journey to learn from each other, network, build something awesome, and even compete for prizes.

Venue

Glass City Hacks will be hosted in the world renowned Toledo Museum of Art, featuring one of the world's most extensive collections of artwork from all corners of the earth.




  • Toledo Museum of Art

    Work in the galleries of the Toledo Museum of Art containing over 30,000 works of art dating from the very recent to thousands of years old!

  • The Glass Pavilion

    Hack in a building of hanging glass panels that blur the boundaries between interior and exterior spaces featuring over 5,000 works of contemporary glass art.

  • The Peristyle

    The Peristyle features seats for 1,750 people in a massive Greek-inspired concert hall where Glass City Hacks will hold the opening and closing ceremonies along with speakers throughout the hackathon.

  • Medieval Cloister

    Work in a medieval style cloister framed by gothic archways and featuring tapestries, stained glass, and other artwork dating back centuries.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a creative learning, building, and networking marathon. Participants are encouraged for form teams and build anything they want over the course of one weekend.

We're bringing together coders, artists, designers, engineers, and others to build clever apps, incredible hardware solutions, and even great works of art. Make literally anything.



  • Who can participate?

    Anyone over the age of 18 can register to participate in Glass City Hacks. You don’t have to attend a university and you don't have to live in or around Toledo in order to attend.

  • How much will Glass City Hacks cost?

    Glass City Hacks is completely free to attend! We’ll also be feeding you for the entire event. Plus lots of snacks, swag and a great experience.

  • Can I start my hackathon project beforehand?

    To be fair to everyone, participants should not be working on their projects before Glass City Hacks. This includes any pre-existing projects. Feel free to use third-party APIs, libraries, and any tools that are openly available. If you're itching to get started, you can prepare by familiarizing yourself with the specific tools or technologies you'll be using in your project.

  • What if I’m totally new and have no experience or existing skillset.

    No worries. It’s definitely a plus to have prior coding, engineering, or artistic skill, but it’s not a requirement. We’ll have talks, mentors and workshops to help you with your project—Hackathons are an exciting place to learn new skills in a short amount of time. Just come eager to learn and excited to meet lots of awesome people..

  • Is there travel reimbursement?

    We fully intend to offer travel reimbursement up to a specified amount as well as intra-university buses that will be picking up participants far and wide and busing them to Toledo and back. A bus may be coming to a city near you. Stay tuned for updates!

  • What food will be provided?

    We’ll be providing you with breakfast, lunch, and dinner as well as frequent snacks and drinks throughout the hackathon.

  • What if I don't have a team or idea?

    Not to worry! There will be many opportunities to network and meet other participants to form teams, and we'll make sure you're not left out in the cold. There will even be workshops, talks, and mentors to help with brainstorming.

  • How many people can be on my team?

    Up to four people can be on a single team. They don't have to come from the same school or city as you and they do not need to be planned ahead of time.

  • What should I bring?

    Bring the essentials, your ID, your cellphone, tablet, any chargers you might need. If you're programming, you'll need a laptop and charger. If you're building a hardware hack, bring all the components you'll need to get building. If you're making art, bring all the materials you'll need to make it. You can also bring a sleeping bag and any toiletries you might need.

  • I have a question that is not listed here. Who should I ask?

    Feel free to reach out to us on Twitter, Facebook or email toledohackathon@gmail.com.
